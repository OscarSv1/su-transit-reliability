Punctuality & delay analysis

34 snapshot days · 6.97M trip-stop observations · January 2023 – July 2026

EXPECTED CHAOS, FOUND RELIABILITY

I built this expecting to find a transit system under strain, chronic delays, bad lines, a daily rush-hour breakdown. The data showed the opposite. SL runs on time. Across every mode, fewer than 1% of trips arrive more than 10 minutes late, and on the metro it is effectively zero.

The failures that do exist aren't systemic. They cluster on specific dates and specific hours  a few hours on a handful of days tied to planned engineering work or one-off incidents, not to any weakness you could optimize away. At 99.5%+ punctuality, there is no structural problem to fix. This dashboard shows what the data actually says.

Method & limitations

SL publishes real-time data as GTFS protobuf through KoDa. I decoded 34 monthly snapshot days spanning 2023–2026, filtered from 161GB to 612MB, loaded to BigQuery, and modelled the transformations in dbt.

Trip-to-line matching

Date-specific static schedules. SL reassigns trip IDs across schedule versions, so a naive join covers only 8.7% of records; date-matched joins reach 79%.

Artifact filtering

Days with implausible failure rates, holiday timetables, and planned engineering work are excluded as schedule mismatches, not delays.

Sample scope

34 discrete days. Supports line and mode-level comparison; does not support seasonal or time-of-day trend claims.

Weather

Cold weather shows a small measurable effect on buses only (0.44% vs 0.36% when freezing), on a thin sample of freezing dates.
